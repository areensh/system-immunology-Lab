Results: Metadata-Driven Cohort Assembly via Cross-Study Querying
=================================================================

1. Dataset Overview
-------------------

Using the iReceptor Statistics API, we queried metadata across seven immune
repertoire datasets stored in a federated database system. A total of 121
subject records were retrieved (Figure 01). The largest dataset was CD1
(Covid19_db3, n=51, PMID: 37153628), followed by CD2 (covid_db2, n=19,
PMID: 33384691), GT1 (sykesIgblast2020, n=15, PMID: 38014202), CD3
(covid19, n=13, PMID: 32669287), CVX1 (vaccine2, n=12, PMID: 34648302),
HC1 (lp16_Igblast, n=6, PMID: 28829438), and CVX2 (covid_vaccine_new,
n=5, PMID: 33858945). Two experimental controls (Fb and Water) in CVX2
and three subjects (D159, D154, Hu-1) in HC1 were excluded as non-subject
or unpublished entries.

2. Metadata Availability
------------------------

To assess the scope of available annotations, we examined four metadata
fields across all datasets: Tissue, Sex, Disease Stage, and Cell Subset
(Figure 00). Tissue and Disease Stage were the most consistently reported
fields. Cell Subset was reported for 20 of 121 subjects across two
datasets (CVX2 and GT1).

3. Selection of Key Clinical Variables
--------------------------------------

Among the available metadata fields, three were identified as most suitable
for cross-study clinical comparison: Disease Stage, Age, and Sex (Figure 04).
These fields offer the best combination of coverage and clinical relevance:

  - Disease Stage was reported for all subjects in 6 of 7 datasets (only
    GT1 lacked this field entirely). This field enables stratification of
    subjects across a clinical severity spectrum.

  - Age was reported for 113 of 121 subjects (93.4%). Missing values were
    concentrated among specific subjects: H3, H4, H8 in CD3 (healthy
    controls); and Cov7 and Cov1 in CD1.

  - Sex was reported for 93 of 121 subjects (76.9%). The entire CD3
    dataset (13 subjects) lacked sex information, as did GT1 (15 subjects).

Cell Subset and Tissue were excluded as stratification variables. Cell Subset
was too sparse for meaningful cross-study comparison. Tissue was nearly
uniform across COVID datasets (blood/peripheral blood), providing no
discriminatory power.

4. Disease Spectrum Coverage and Cohort Assembly
------------------------------------------------

To construct a cohort spanning the full clinical spectrum of COVID-19, we
harmonized disease stage labels across studies into six broad categories:
Healthy, COVID Naive, Mild, Moderate, Severe, and Recovered. The raw disease
stage labels were mapped as follows:

  Healthy:    "healthy" (CD3, HC1)
  COVID Naive: "COVID Naive" (CVX1)
  Mild:       "mild" (CD1), "non-severe" (CD3)
  Moderate:   "Early phase-Stable", "Early phase-Improving" (CD2)
  Severe:     "severe" (CD1, CD3), "Early phase hypoxaemia" (CD2)
  Recovered:  "Recovered" (CVX2), "COVID recovered" (CVX1),
              "Recovering post-ICU", "Recovering without ICU" (CD2)

Figure 05 reveals a critical insight: the COVID-19 datasets alone provided
only 3 healthy controls (from CD3). This is insufficient for robust
comparison against disease groups. However, the HC1 dataset — a non-COVID
immune repertoire study of healthy donors — contributed 6 additional healthy
subjects with reported age and sex data. By including HC1, the healthy
control group increased from 3 to 9 subjects, demonstrating the strength
of cross-study metadata querying: the ability to identify and incorporate
complementary datasets that fill gaps in any individual study's design.

The GT1 dataset (n=15) was excluded from the assembled cohort because it
lacked Disease Stage and Sex metadata, making it unsuitable for the chosen
stratification variables.

5. Assembled Cohort
--------------------

The final cohort comprises 106 subjects from 6 datasets, spanning the full
disease spectrum (Figure 06):

  - Healthy:     9 subjects (6 from HC1, 3 from CD3)
  - COVID Naive:  8 subjects (CVX1)
  - Mild:        41 subjects (39 from CD1, 2 from CD3)
  - Moderate:     9 subjects (CD2)
  - Severe:      27 subjects (12 CD1, 8 CD3, 7 CD2)
  - Recovered:   12 subjects (5 CVX2, 4 CVX1, 3 CD2)

Three disease categories — Severe, Recovered, and Healthy — are represented
by subjects from multiple independent studies, strengthening the
generalizability of downstream comparisons. The Mild category is dominated by
CD1 (39/41), while Moderate and COVID Naive each come from a single study.

This assembled cohort was then used for demographic characterization and
cross-tabulation analyses of disease stage by age and sex.
