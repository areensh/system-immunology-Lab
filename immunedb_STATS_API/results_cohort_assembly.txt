Results: Metadata-Driven Cohort Assembly via Cross-Study Querying
=================================================================

1. Dataset Overview
-------------------

Using the iReceptor Statistics API, we queried metadata across seven immune
repertoire datasets stored in a federated database system. A total of 124
subject records were retrieved (Figure 01). The largest dataset was Covid19_db3
(n=51, PMID: 37153628), followed by covid_db2 (n=19, PMID: 33384691),
sykesIgblast2020 (n=15, PMID: 38014202), covid19 (n=13, PMID: 32669287),
vaccine2 (n=12, PMID: 34648302), lp16_Igblast (n=9, PMID: 28829438), and
covid_vaccine_new (n=5, PMID: 33858945). Two experimental controls (Fb and
Water) in the covid_vaccine_new dataset were excluded as non-subject entries.

2. Metadata Availability
------------------------

To assess the scope of available annotations, we examined five metadata fields
across all datasets: Tissue, Sex, Disease Stage, Cell Subset, and Age
(Figure 00). Tissue and Disease Stage were the most consistently reported
fields. Cell Subset was the sparsest, reported for only 21 of 126 subjects
across three datasets (covid_vaccine_new, lp16_Igblast, sykesIgblast2020).
Note: the two experimental controls (Fb and Water) in covid_vaccine_new were
excluded from all analyses.

3. Selection of Key Clinical Variables
--------------------------------------

Among the available metadata fields, three were identified as most suitable
for cross-study clinical comparison: Disease Stage, Age, and Sex (Figure 04).
These fields offer the best combination of coverage and clinical relevance:

  - Disease Stage was reported for all subjects in 6 of 7 datasets (only
    sykesIgblast2020 lacked this field entirely). This field enables
    stratification of subjects across a clinical severity spectrum.

  - Age was reported for 113 of 124 subjects (91.1%). Missing values were
    concentrated among specific subjects: H3, H4, H8 in covid19 (healthy
    controls); Cov7 and Cov1 in Covid19_db3; and D159, D154, and Hu-1 in
    lp16_Igblast.

  - Sex was reported for 105 of 124 subjects (84.7%). The entire covid19
    dataset (13 subjects) lacked sex information, as did sykesIgblast2020
    (15 subjects), and 3 subjects in lp16_Igblast (D159, D154, Hu-1).

Cell Subset and Tissue were excluded as stratification variables. Cell Subset
was too sparse for meaningful cross-study comparison. Tissue was nearly
uniform across COVID datasets (blood/peripheral blood), providing no
discriminatory power.

4. Disease Spectrum Coverage and Cohort Assembly
------------------------------------------------

To construct a cohort spanning the full clinical spectrum of COVID-19, we
harmonized disease stage labels across studies into six broad categories:
Healthy, COVID Naive, Mild, Moderate, Severe, and Recovered. The raw disease
stage labels were mapped as follows:

  Healthy:    "healthy" (covid19, lp16_Igblast)
  COVID Naive: "COVID Naive" (vaccine2)
  Mild:       "mild" (Covid19_db3), "non-severe" (covid19)
  Moderate:   "Early phase-Stable", "Early phase-Improving" (covid_db2)
  Severe:     "severe" (Covid19_db3, covid19), "Early phase hypoxaemia" (covid_db2)
  Recovered:  "Recovered" (covid_vaccine_new), "COVID recovered" (vaccine2),
              "Recovering post-ICU", "Recovering without ICU" (covid_db2)

Figure 05 reveals a critical insight: the COVID-19 datasets alone provided
only 3 healthy controls (from covid19). This is insufficient for robust
comparison against disease groups. However, the lp16_Igblast dataset — a
non-COVID immune repertoire study of healthy donors — contributed 9 additional
healthy subjects with reported age (6/9) and sex (6/9) data. By including
lp16_Igblast, the healthy control group increased from 3 to 12 subjects,
demonstrating the strength of cross-study metadata querying: the ability to
identify and incorporate complementary datasets that fill gaps in any
individual study's design.

The sykesIgblast2020 dataset (n=15) was excluded from the assembled cohort
because it lacked Disease Stage and Sex metadata, making it unsuitable for
the chosen stratification variables.

5. Assembled Cohort
--------------------

The final cohort comprises 109 subjects from 6 datasets, spanning the full
disease spectrum (Figure 06):

  - Healthy:     12 subjects (9 from lp16_Igblast, 3 from covid19)
  - COVID Naive:  8 subjects (vaccine2)
  - Mild:        41 subjects (39 from Covid19_db3, 2 from covid19)
  - Moderate:     9 subjects (covid_db2)
  - Severe:      27 subjects (12 Covid19_db3, 8 covid19, 7 covid_db2)
  - Recovered:   12 subjects (5 covid_vaccine_new, 4 vaccine2, 3 covid_db2)

Three disease categories — Severe, Recovered, and Healthy — are represented
by subjects from multiple independent studies, strengthening the
generalizability of downstream comparisons. The Mild category is dominated by
Covid19_db3 (39/41), while Moderate and COVID Naive each come from a single
study.

This assembled cohort was then used for demographic characterization and
cross-tabulation analyses of disease stage by age and sex.
