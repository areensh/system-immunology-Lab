============================================================
RESULTS — Metadata-Driven Cohort Assembly
============================================================

1. Dataset Overview and Metadata Availability
----------------------------------------------

Using the iReceptor Statistics API, we queried metadata across seven
immune repertoire datasets stored in a federated database system. A total
of 121 subject records were retrieved from seven independent datasets:
CD1 (n=51), CD2 (n=19), GT1 (n=15), CD3 (n=13), CVX1 (n=12), HC1 (n=6),
and CVX2 (n=5) (Figure 1). Two experimental controls (Fb, Water) in CVX2
and three subjects (D159, D154, Hu-1) in HC1 were excluded as non-subject
or unpublished entries.

We examined five metadata fields across all datasets: Tissue, Disease
Stage, Sex, Cell Subset, and Age (Figure 2). Tissue and Disease Stage were
the most consistently reported fields. CD2 and CVX1 had the most complete
metadata, with all subjects reporting Disease Stage, Sex, and Tissue. CD3
(n=13) lacked sex annotations entirely, and GT1 (n=15) lacked both Disease
Stage and Sex. Cell Subset was reported for only 20 of 121 subjects,
limited to CVX2 and GT1. Age was available for 113 of 121 subjects (93.4%),
with missing values concentrated among the 3 healthy controls in CD3 and
2 subjects in CD1.

2. Disease Stage Heterogeneity
-------------------------------

Examination of disease stage annotations across the six datasets with
reported disease information revealed substantial heterogeneity in labeling
conventions (Figure 3). A total of 13 distinct disease stage labels were
used across 106 subjects (excluding GT1, which lacked disease stage data).
The Severe end of the spectrum was represented by "severe" (n=20, from CD1
and CD3) and "Early phase hypoxaemia" (n=7, CD2). Mild disease was labeled
as "mild" (n=39, CD1) or "non-severe" (n=2, CD3). Moderate disease appeared
only in CD2 under "Early phase-Stable" (n=8) and "Early phase-Improving"
(n=1). The recovery spectrum showed the greatest label diversity, with five
distinct labels originating from three studies: "Recovered" (n=5, CVX2),
"COVID recovered" (n=4, CVX1), "Recovering post-ICU" (n=1), "Recovering
post-ICU -Improving" (n=1), and "Recovering without ICU-Improving" (n=1,
all CD2). Healthy subjects were labeled as "healthy" in both CD3 (n=3) and
HC1 (n=6), and COVID-naive subjects as "COVID Naive" (n=8, CVX1).

3. Cross-Study Cohort Assembly
-------------------------------

To construct a cohort spanning the full clinical spectrum of COVID-19, we
harmonized the 13 raw disease stage labels into six broad categories:
Healthy, COVID Naive, Mild, Moderate, Severe, and Recovered. GT1 was
excluded from the cohort because it lacked Disease Stage and Sex metadata,
making it unsuitable for the chosen stratification variables.

A critical gap emerged during this process: the COVID-19 datasets alone
provided only 3 healthy controls, all from CD3 — a number insufficient
for robust comparison against disease groups. However, this is precisely
where cross-study metadata querying demonstrates its strength. By querying
disease stage metadata across all available datasets, we identified that
the HC1 dataset — a non-COVID immune repertoire study — contained 6
additional healthy donors with complete age and sex annotations (Figure 4).
Incorporating HC1 as the primary source of healthy controls, and excluding
the 3 CD3 healthy subjects (which also lacked age and sex data), the
assembled cohort comprises 103 subjects from 6 datasets spanning the full
disease spectrum.

The harmonized disease categories with their contributing datasets and
original labels are shown in Figure 5:

  Mild (n=41):       "mild" (n=39, CD1), "non-severe" (n=2, CD3)
  Severe (n=27):     "severe" (n=20, CD1/CD3), "Early phase hypoxaemia"
                     (n=7, CD2)
  Recovered (n=12):  "Recovered" (n=5, CVX2), "COVID recovered" (n=4,
                     CVX1), "Recovering post-ICU" and variants (n=3, CD2)
  Moderate (n=9):    "Early phase-Stable" (n=8), "Early phase-Improving"
                     (n=1, both CD2)
  COVID Naive (n=8): "COVID Naive" (n=8, CVX1)
  Healthy (n=6):     "healthy" (n=6, HC1)

The Mild and Severe categories constitute the two largest groups (68/103,
66%), providing sufficient statistical power for pairwise comparisons.
Three disease categories — Severe, Recovered, and Healthy — are represented
by subjects from multiple independent studies, strengthening the
generalizability of downstream analyses.

4. Cohort Demographics
-----------------------

Age data were available for 101 of 103 subjects (2 missing from CD1),
with ages ranging from 18 to 87.4 years (median = 48, mean = 47.6)
(Figure 6). Sex was reported for 92 of 103 subjects; 57 males and 35
females. The 11 subjects with missing sex data originate from CD3 (n=10)
and CD1 (n=1).

Males were overrepresented in the Severe group (14 males vs. 5 females,
with 8 of unknown sex), whereas the Mild group showed an equal sex
distribution (19 males, 19 females, 3 unknown). The COVID Naive group
was evenly split (4 males, 4 females), and the Healthy group from HC1
consisted of 5 males and 1 female.

Participants with Severe disease were notably older (median age 61 years)
compared to those with Mild disease (median 48.5 years), consistent with
the well-documented association between age and COVID-19 severity. The
COVID Naive group was the youngest (median 20 years), and the Recovered
group was also relatively young (median 30.5 years), while the Moderate
group was older (median 51.7 years).

Participants with missing metadata for a given variable were excluded from
the respective downstream analyses.


============================================================
FIGURE CAPTIONS
============================================================

Figure 1. Distribution of subjects across datasets.
Bar chart showing the number of subjects contributed by each of the seven
datasets included in the analysis: CD1 (n=51), CD2 (n=19), GT1 (n=15),
CD3 (n=13), CVX1 (n=12), HC1 (n=6), and CVX2 (n=5), for a total of 121
subjects.

Figure 2. Metadata field availability per dataset.
Grouped bar chart showing the number of subjects with reported values for
five metadata types — Tissue, Disease Stage, Sex, Cell Subset, and Age —
across all seven datasets. Tissue and Disease Stage are the most
consistently reported fields. GT1 lacks Disease Stage and Sex; CD3 lacks
Sex; Cell Subset is limited to CVX2 and GT1.

Figure 3. Raw disease stage labels across datasets.
Stacked bar chart showing the distribution of the 13 original disease
stage labels used across six datasets (excluding GT1, which lacked disease
stage annotations). Each bar represents one disease stage label, colored
by the contributing dataset. The heterogeneity of labels across studies
motivates harmonization for cross-study comparison.

Figure 4. Disease spectrum coverage highlighting cross-study gap filling.
Bar chart showing the number of subjects per harmonized disease category,
distinguishing contributions from COVID-19 datasets (orange) and the
non-COVID HC1 dataset (green). HC1 provides the sole source of healthy
controls (n=6) in the assembled cohort, demonstrating the value of
cross-study metadata querying for identifying complementary datasets.
Three CD3 healthy subjects were excluded due to missing age and sex data.

Figure 5. Harmonized disease categories with original label provenance.
Stacked bar chart of the six harmonized disease categories, with bars
colored by contributing dataset and original disease stage labels annotated
within each segment. This figure maps the 13 raw labels to 6 harmonized
categories while preserving traceability to the source studies.

Figure 6. Subject demographics by disease category.
Scatter plot showing the age and sex distribution of subjects across
harmonized disease categories, colored by dataset. Each point represents
one subject; shape indicates sex (circle = male, triangle = female,
cross = unknown). Subjects with Severe disease tend to be older (median
61 years) and predominantly male, while COVID Naive and Recovered groups
are younger. Of 103 subjects, 101 had reported age and 92 had reported sex.
