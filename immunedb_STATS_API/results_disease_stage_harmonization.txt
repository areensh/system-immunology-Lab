Results: Disease Stage Heterogeneity and Harmonization
=======================================================

1. Raw Disease Stage Labels Across Studies
------------------------------------------

Examination of disease stage annotations across the six datasets with
reported disease information revealed substantial heterogeneity in
labeling conventions (Figure 07a). A total of 13 distinct disease stage
labels were used across the datasets, reflecting differences in clinical
classification schemes between studies.

The Severe end of the spectrum was represented by three labels: "severe"
(n=20, from Covid19_db3 and covid19) and "Early phase hypoxaemia" (n=7,
from covid_db2). Mild disease was labeled as "mild" (n=39, Covid19_db3)
or "non-severe" (n=2, covid19). Moderate disease appeared only in
covid_db2 under two labels: "Early phase-Stable" (n=8) and "Early
phase-Improving" (n=1).

The recovery spectrum showed the greatest label diversity, with five
distinct labels all originating from different studies: "Recovering
post-ICU" (n=1), "Recovering post-ICU -Improving" (n=1), "Recovering
without ICU-Improving" (n=1) from covid_db2; "Recovered" (n=5) from
covid_vaccine_new; and "COVID recovered" (n=4) from vaccine2. Healthy
subjects were labeled as "healthy" (n=12, from covid19 and lp16_Igblast),
and COVID-naive subjects as "COVID Naive" (n=8, vaccine2).

This heterogeneity is a direct consequence of independent study designs
and highlights the challenge — and necessity — of metadata harmonization
when assembling cross-study cohorts.

2. Harmonized Disease Categories
---------------------------------

To enable meaningful cross-study comparison, the 13 raw labels were
mapped into six harmonized categories representing a clinical severity
spectrum (Figure 07b):

  Severe (n=27):
    - "severe" (n=20) — Covid19_db3, covid19
    - "Early phase hypoxaemia" (n=7) — covid_db2

  Mild (n=41):
    - "mild" (n=39) — Covid19_db3
    - "non-severe" (n=2) — covid19

  Moderate (n=9):
    - "Early phase-Stable" (n=8) — covid_db2
    - "Early phase-Improving" (n=1) — covid_db2

  Recovered (n=12):
    - "Recovered" (n=5) — covid_vaccine_new
    - "COVID recovered" (n=4) — vaccine2
    - "Recovering post-ICU" (n=1) — covid_db2
    - "Recovering post-ICU -Improving" (n=1) — covid_db2
    - "Recovering without ICU-Improving" (n=1) — covid_db2

  Healthy (n=12):
    - "healthy" (n=12) — covid19 (3), lp16_Igblast (9)

  COVID Naive (n=8):
    - "COVID Naive" (n=8) — vaccine2

The harmonization preserves the original labels as traceable sub-categories
while enabling aggregation across the clinical spectrum. The Mild category
is predominantly from a single study (Covid19_db3, 39/41), whereas Severe,
Recovered, and Healthy draw from multiple independent datasets, providing
more robust representation. The Recovered category, despite its small total
(n=12), spans three independent studies and five original labels, reflecting
the diversity of recovery trajectories captured across the federated data.
